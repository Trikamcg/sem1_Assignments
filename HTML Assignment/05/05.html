Masala Dosa

Masala dosa is a famous South Indian dish made with fermented rice-lentil batter and a spiced potato filling, served crisp with chutney and sambar.

Ingredients

  • Rice-lentil batter (idli/dosa batter)
  • Potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • Onions (sliced)
  • Oil or ghee
    • Neutral oil
    • Ghee (for richer flavor)
  • Spices and aromatics
    • Mustard seeds
    • Curry leaves
    • Green chilies
    • Turmeric
    • Salt
  • Optional accompaniments
    • Coconut chutney
    • Sambar

Cooking Steps

  1. Heat oil, splutter mustard seeds, add curry leaves and green chilies; sauté onions until translucent.
  2. Stir in turmeric and mashed potatoes; season with salt and mix well to make the masala filling.
  3. Heat a flat pan (tawa); pour a ladle of batter and spread in a circular motion into a thin dosa.
  4. Drizzle a few drops of oil or ghee around the edges; cook until the bottom turns golden.
  5. Place a portion of potato masala in the center; fold or roll the dosa over the filling.
  6. Repeat with remaining batter and filling, adjusting heat as needed for crispness.
  7. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Cooking Terms

Sauté
Cooking quickly over medium heat in a small amount of oil or fat.
Tempering (Tadka)
Blooming whole spices in hot fat to release aroma and flavor.
Ferment
Allowing batter to rest so natural microbes produce gas and tang for better texture.

Paneer Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala is a creamy, mildly spiced tomato-based curry featuring tender paneer cubes enriched with butter and cream.

Ingredients

  • Paneer cubes
  • Tomatoes (pureed)
  • Onions (finely chopped)
  • Butter or ghee
    • Unsalted butter
    • Ghee (for a nutty aroma)
  • Spices
    • Garam masala
    • Red chili powder
    • Coriander powder
    • Cumin
    • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek)
  • Cream (heavy or fresh)
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt and sugar (to balance)

Cooking Steps

  1. Lightly pan-fry paneer cubes in a little butter; set aside.
  2. In the same pan, melt butter, sauté onions until golden, then add ginger-garlic paste and cook off raw aroma.
  3. Add tomato puree; simmer until the mixture thickens and the fat begins to separate.
  4. Stir in spices (garam masala, chili, coriander, cumin) and kasuri methi; cook 1–2 minutes.
  5. Add cream and adjust seasoning with salt and a pinch of sugar; blend if a smoother gravy is desired.
  6. Return paneer cubes to the gravy; simmer gently for 3–5 minutes to absorb flavors.
  7. Finish with a small knob of butter and serve hot with naan or rice.

Cooking Terms

Simmer
Cook gently at just below boiling to meld flavors without over-reducing.
Purée
Blend ingredients into a smooth, uniform paste or sauce.
Deglaze
Loosen browned bits from a pan with liquid to enrich a sauce.

Gulab Jamun

Gulab Jamun is a classic Indian dessert of khoya-based dough balls, deep-fried until golden and soaked in fragrant sugar syrup.

Ingredients

  • Khoya (mawa), crumbled
  • Maida (all-purpose flour)
  • Baking powder or baking soda (a pinch)
  • Ghee or neutral oil for frying
    • Ghee (traditional flavor)
    • Refined oil (neutral alternative)
  • Sugar syrup
    • Sugar
    • Water
    • Cardamom pods
    • Rose water or kewra
    • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Chopped pistachios or almonds (for garnish, optional)

Cooking Steps

  1. Make the syrup: boil sugar and water, add cardamom and rose water; keep warm off heat.
  2. Combine khoya, maida, and a pinch of leavening; gently knead to a soft, crack-free dough.
  3. Divide and roll into smooth, small balls without cracks.
  4. Heat ghee/oil on low-medium; fry balls slowly, stirring gently, until evenly golden brown.
  5. Drain briefly, then add warm jamuns to warm syrup; soak at least 30 minutes.
  6. Garnish with nuts and serve warm or at room temperature.
  7. For softer texture, ensure low frying temperature and adequate soaking time.

Cooking Terms

Knead
Work dough with hands to develop structure and achieve smoothness.
Syrup
A concentrated sugar solution, often flavored, used for soaking or glazing.
Proof/Rest
Allow dough or formed items to relax, improving texture before cooking.
